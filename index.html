How Animals Deal with Death

Some animals treat the corpses of community members in specific ways. For example, in social insects, like ants and termites, the dead are eaten, buried or removed from the colony, depending on the cause and context of the death. Corvid birds, including magpies and crows, gather around their deceased, as if having a funeral. And mothers have been seen carrying dead infants for hours to weeks in diverse species, like elephants, whales and dingos.
